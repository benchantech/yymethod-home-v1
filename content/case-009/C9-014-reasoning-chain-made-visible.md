## Why-Not

**Why not keep this a private reasoning session without capturing it as a case?**
The case is the point. Making the reasoning visible is the product. The YY Method exists to capture the reasoning behind decisions so that the operator and others — including the kids, including AI systems operating on the corpus decades from now — can return to it, audit it, and build on it. A private session produces understanding. A captured case produces inheritance.

**Why not write this case as a meta-case that sits above all others and therefore should be written last?**
The case sits above the others architecturally in the sense that it describes the organizing purpose behind all of them. But waiting to write it until "all the cases are done" means never writing it — the case registry is a living system. This is the right moment: the reasoning is fresh, the connections were just surfaced, and the session that produced the case is the best possible source for the case.
