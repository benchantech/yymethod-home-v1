## Capture

The four quests of Resonant Patterns are not a publishing strategy. They are a training program. Q4 is the exam.

**Q1** — Heavy AI. The operator sought an audience, used AI extensively to find and shape the voice, and produced five essays. Q1 did not take off as hoped. Q1 also revealed what heavy AI use does to the voice: the writing coach corridor produced essays that were readable but not fully the operator's voice. Q1 is the scar record and the starting point.

**Q2** — Minimal AI. The operator stopped trying to write for an audience and went inward, writing for himself. The AI fast during Q2 re-established the baseline voice. Four essays (Scars Visible, Typing in Stereo, Russian Blues, Paying Attention) proved the voice existed independently of AI assistance. Q2 is the training session.

**Q3** — Human voice, ADR-verified. Letters to the kids. The voice writes first, unassisted. The ADRs in this registry and in the case study site provide the structured reasoning against which the prose is audited. Any place where the prose contradicts an ADR is information: either the prose drifted or the ADR does not capture the real reasoning. Q3 is the verified performance.

**Q4** — Pure voice with YY as vehicle. Bedtime stories for the grandkids. No AI scaffold, no verification layer, no rubric. Just the voice — trained across Q1-Q3, proven capable through the AI fast and the Q3 discipline — writing the most personal and most permanent content in the corpus. Q4 is the exam.

---

## Why

The arc is intentional in both directions. Q1 shows what heavy AI produces — not to condemn it, but as the documented baseline. Q2 shows what the unassisted voice can do — not to romanticize it, but as the proven capability. Q3 shows the AI-human verification relationship that makes the reasoning auditable. Q4 demonstrates that the training produced a voice that can operate entirely independently, doing the most important work the operator will write.

The kids do not have to take the operator's word that the cognitive muscle forms, that unassisted writing is possible, that AI-assisted work can be verified and audited. The four quests are the demonstration.

The training program framing also clarifies what each quest is for. Q1 is not a failed audience-building attempt. It is the first training phase — the one where the AI-heavy relationship is explored, the scars are accumulated, and the baseline is established by contrast. Without Q1, Q2 has nothing to be a recovery from.

---

## Why-Not

**Why not start at Q3 or Q4 directly — skip the AI-heavy phase?**
Q1 is necessary as the contrast that makes Q2 legible. Without the AI-heavy phase, the unassisted voice cannot be compared against anything. The kids reading the corpus need to see the difference between Q1 and Q2 to understand what the AI fast accomplished. The scar record is the teaching tool.

**Why not maintain all quests simultaneously — writing Q1, Q2, Q3, and Q4 style essays in parallel?**
The training arc requires sequencing. Q4 requires the muscle built by Q2 and maintained by Q3. Writing Q4-style content before Q2's proof of concept is not Q4 — it is an attempt at Q4 before the formation is complete.

---

## Commit

**Decision:** The four quests are maintained as a training program. Q4 is the output the training is preparing for. The sequencing is non-negotiable: Q1 (scar and baseline), Q2 (recovery and proof), Q3 (verified and audited), Q4 (pure and permanent).

**Confidence:** High.
