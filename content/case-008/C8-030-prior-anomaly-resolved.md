## Capture

Before the current year's event, the scoring rules were explicitly clarified — specifically the rule that premium square multipliers may only be applied once and are exhausted after first use. This clarification was not provoked by a formal challenge or complaint. It was a governance update to the event format, presumably to ensure consistent understanding among participants.

This clarification constitutes retroactive structural evidence bearing on the prior year's anomalous result.

---

## Why

The charitable hypothesis in C8-004 held that the prior anomalous result was most likely produced by accidental misapplication of the premium square rule — a rule that, it now turns out, was not explicitly stated before play in prior years. The fact that the organizers deemed this rule worth clarifying explicitly confirms that it was previously ambiguous or inconsistently applied. A rule that needed clarifying is a rule that was not reliably enforced.

This closes the prior anomaly without requiring any accusation. The reasoning chain is complete:

1. The prior result appeared implausible under proper rule application (C8-008).
2. The charitable hypothesis attributed it to accidental multiplier reuse (C8-004).
3. The rule governing multiplier use was subsequently clarified by the organizers.
4. The clarification confirms the rule was previously unclear — consistent with accidental misapplication.
5. The prior result is explained. The matter is closed.

The commitment not to relitigate (C8-023) holds in full. The record is updated, not reopened.

---

## Why-Not

**Why not treat the clarification as confirmation that deliberate misapplication occurred?**
The clarification confirms ambiguity, not intent. An ambiguous rule produces accidental errors by default. Inferring deliberate misapplication from an ambiguous rule requires additional evidence that does not exist. The charitable hypothesis remains the correct reading.

**Why not revisit the prior result now that the rule is formally established?**
The result cannot be retroactively adjudicated. It stands as recorded. The value of the clarification is forward-looking: it prevents the same ambiguity from producing the same errors. The record of the prior anomaly is preserved in this case as context, not as a grievance to be resolved.

---

## Commit

**Decision:** The prior year's anomalous result is attributed to accidental rule misapplication under an unclarified format, consistent with C8-004. The subsequent rule clarification by the organizers confirms this attribution structurally. The integrity question is resolved. The case moves forward.

**Confidence:** High. The structural evidence is now complete.
