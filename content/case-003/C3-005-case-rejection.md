## Capture

Case 003 was reviewed against the registry's own standard and found insufficient. Four ADRs were produced. The case was left in "In Progress" status. The essays it was meant to accompany were not written. The energy to continue never arrived.

That absence is a signal. When a case is genuinely strong, the reasoning accumulates naturally — decisions build on decisions, scars surface, domains fill out. When a case stalls at four entries with no clear path forward, the stall is a verdict.

The case is rejected. It stays in the registry.

## Why

Four ADRs is not a case. It is a start that didn't compound. The title — "Quest 3 — Cognitive Theft & AI Governance" — carries weight that four thin writing-process records cannot support. A visitor who reads the title and clicks through encounters a mismatch: the promise of a governance framework, the delivery of essay scaffolding notes.

The mismatch is a credibility cost to the registry. A registry that holds weak cases and calls them "In Progress" indefinitely is a registry that has stopped applying the method to itself.

The method applies to the registry. The same standard that governs whether a decision is well-formed governs whether a case earns its place.

---

## Why-Not

**Why not expand Case 003 to meet the standard?**
The expansion would be performed rather than genuine. The four existing ADRs captured what was live and pressurized at the time. Returning to the case now to manufacture additional ADRs — to reach some minimum count — is exactly the documentation trap the method exists to prevent. If the reasoning were alive, it would have accumulated already.

**Why not delete the case?**
Deletion removes the evidence. The scar record is more useful than a clean registry. A visitor who sees a rejected case with this ADR attached understands that the registry is curated, that cases are evaluated after the fact, and that the operator holds the registry to the same standard as the decisions it documents. That understanding is not available from a clean registry.

**Why not leave it as "In Progress" indefinitely?**
"In Progress" is a claim: work is happening. No work is happening. Labeling a stalled case "In Progress" is a small lie that accumulates into a larger credibility problem. The honest label is "Rejected." The honest preservation is the scar.
