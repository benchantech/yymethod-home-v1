## Capture

Several metaphors emerged naturally:

- stand partner;
- open pages;
- books;
- page turning;
- seeds;
- garden;
- tree;
- embers;
- memory bank;
- ritual.

Each metaphor carried useful meaning, but combining them could produce semantic overload.

Use distinct metaphoric systems for distinct products.

## Why

This demonstrates active semantic architecture rather than accidental branding.

---

## Why-Not

The same user should not be asked to think simultaneously in terms of:

- pages for practice;
- seeds for decisions;
- plants for branches;
- embers for memory;
- stands for trust.

## Commit

**Decision:** Use distinct metaphoric systems for distinct products.

The products can share doctrine without sharing every metaphor.

**Confidence:** Medium-high for public architecture; implementation details remain private and subject to launch evidence.
