## Capture

Delay the move by approximately one year.

This is primarily a financial accumulation window. Not a school year timing decision. Not a patience exercise for family alignment. Not a hedge against uncertainty. The purpose is specific: save enough capital to execute the move without a bank loan, without a family loan, without answering to anyone.

The one year buys sovereignty.

---

## Why

The funding trilemma in C5-007 identified the core problem: the move cannot be done alone without accumulating more capital. Bank loan opposed on principle. Selling the anchor destroys the legacy option. Family loan introduces external obligation.

The resolution is to remove the problem rather than pick the least bad option among the three. Accumulating for approximately one year closes the gap — enough capital to execute without a lender, without a family member, without anyone having leverage over the timing or terms of the decision.

"Answer to no one" is the goal. That phrase is not rhetorical. It means: when the right opportunity appears, the decision belongs entirely to the household. No bank's appraisal requirements. No family member's expectations about repayment timing. No external party whose approval or cooperation is needed before acting.

The year is not passive. It is an active, deliberate accumulation window. The capital being saved is the price of the freedom to move on the operator's terms.

---

## Why-Not

**Why not wait longer to accumulate even more?**
More accumulation reduces risk but also has a real cost: time. The kids' school timelines are real — a child in the right grade for a clean school transition is in that window for one year only. Family alignment, once it arrives, can also drift if the decision is deferred too long. The year is a judgment call about the right balance between accumulation and timeliness.

**Why not start the move now and manage the financing constraint?**
Managing the financing constraint means choosing from the trilemma — bank loan, sell anchor, or family loan. All three are rejected or highly undesirable. Starting now without solving the financing problem means starting from a position of dependency. That is not the model.

**Why not use the year for family alignment instead of capital accumulation?**
Family alignment is a parallel process — it happens through the active-readiness practices of C5-010, not through a dedicated alignment period. Alignment cannot be scheduled. Capital accumulation can. The year is structured around what can be controlled: saving.

---

## Commit

**Decision:** Wait approximately one year. Use the time to accumulate capital sufficient to execute the move with full financial independence — no bank loan, no family loan, no external party involved in the decision. Sovereignty is the goal. The wait is not passive; it is active accumulation toward a clean execution.

**Confidence:** High.
