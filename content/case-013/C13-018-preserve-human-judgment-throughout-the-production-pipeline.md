# C13-018: Preserve Human Judgment Throughout the Production Pipeline

**Method:** YY Method™ Home Edition v2.3 — Capture → Why → Why-Not → Commit → Timestamp

**Status:** Decided

**Date:** 2026-07-24

**Domain:** Human–AI Collaboration

**Depends On:** C13-017

**Freshness Boundary:** Active. Revisit only if a system can independently establish and remain accountable to the human purpose of the work.

**Source:** Public-safe synthesis from iterative AI video production. Exact prompts, generated clips, reference libraries, and platform-specific production fixtures remain private.

---

## Capture

The workflow eventually produced clips that were technically successful with increasing consistency.

Characters remained stable. Props were correct. Motion was believable. The prompt had been followed.

Some of those clips still did not belong.

A visually attractive shot could communicate the wrong relationship, weaken the sequence, draw attention to the wrong subject, or satisfy every instruction while missing the reason the scene existed.

The remaining failures were editorial rather than generative.

---

## Why

As execution improved, fuller automation appeared increasingly plausible.

If story structure, references, geometry, assets, prompts, and experiments could all be systematized, perhaps successful outputs could advance through the pipeline automatically.

---

## Why-Not

Technical correctness cannot determine creative necessity.

The system can generate alternatives and execute constraints. It cannot own the audience relationship, decide which meaning deserves emphasis, or accept responsibility for whether the work serves its purpose.

The final scar was recognizing that removing unnecessary decisions from the generator does not remove the human. It reveals the decision that was human all along.

---

## Commit

**Decision:** Automate execution while preserving human judgment at every editorial boundary.

AI may generate, vary, refine, stabilize, and accelerate. The human remains responsible for deciding:

- what the scene means;
- whether the audience can read it;
- whether it belongs;
- whether another approach better serves the work;
- whether the work should exist in this form at all.

The production system is successful not when the human disappears, but when the human is no longer spending judgment on decisions that can be safely fixed elsewhere.

Case 013 therefore ends where the YY Method begins: human first, AI second. Outsource execution. Never outsource judgment.
