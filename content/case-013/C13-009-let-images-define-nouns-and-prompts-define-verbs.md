# C13-009: Let Images Define Nouns and Prompts Define Verbs

**Method:** YY Method™ Home Edition v2.3 — Capture → Why → Why-Not → Commit → Timestamp

**Status:** Decided

**Date:** 2026-07-24

**Domain:** Prompt Architecture

**Depends On:** C13-008

**Freshness Boundary:** Active. Revisit if future interfaces provide a more reliable explicit separation between identity, scene state, and motion.

**Source:** Public-safe synthesis from iterative AI video production. Exact prompts, generated clips, reference libraries, and platform-specific production fixtures remain private.

---

## Capture

After reference images were introduced, prompts continued to repeat the appearance of the woman, child, clothing, kitchen, violin, and furniture.

The prompts became longer without becoming more reliable. Slight differences between the text and image sometimes altered details that had already been approved.

---

## Why

Repeating important information appeared to reinforce it.

Agreement between the reference and prompt seemed safer than relying on either one alone.

---

## Why-Not

The image had already fixed the nouns.

Restating them required the generator to reconcile two descriptions of the same visual facts. Perfect agreement added little; imperfect agreement reopened settled decisions.

The scar was learning that redundancy can become conflict when two control surfaces describe the same thing.

---

## Commit

**Decision:** Let reference images define the visual nouns of the scene and let the prompt primarily define verbs.

Use imagery to hold people, props, environments, wardrobe, and composition. Use text to direct actions, interactions, timing, objectives, and camera behavior.

Do not restate fixed visual details unless they are intended to change.

The reference image is not only an identity container, however. Its pose and composition also imply motion. The next decision accounts for that.
