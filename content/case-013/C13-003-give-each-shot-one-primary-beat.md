# C13-003: Give Each Shot One Primary Beat

**Method:** YY Method™ Home Edition v2.3 — Capture → Why → Why-Not → Commit → Timestamp

**Status:** Decided

**Date:** 2026-07-24

**Domain:** Temporal Architecture

**Depends On:** C13-002

**Freshness Boundary:** Active. Revisit if future systems reliably preserve several simultaneous story objectives in a short shot without reducing clarity.

**Source:** Public-safe synthesis from iterative AI video production. Exact prompts, generated clips, reference libraries, and platform-specific production fixtures remain private.

---

## Capture

Reducing the sequence to four shots did not solve the problem by itself.

A lesson shot still attempted to show the teacher instructing, the child playing, the parent understanding, the violin technique, and the room relationship. A doorway shot attempted to show gratitude, packing, waving, turning, leaving, and carrying the instrument.

The sequence was smaller, but each shot remained crowded.

---

## Why

Once the number of shots was limited, combining actions seemed efficient.

A richer shot appeared more cinematic, and each generation felt expensive enough that every frame should perform several jobs.

---

## Why-Not

Multiple primary actions competed for visual priority.

The generator produced several incomplete gestures instead of one readable event. The viewer had to decide where to look and which movement represented the story.

The third scar was discovering that a short shot cannot become clear merely by containing the right ingredients. It must declare one dominant event.

---

## Commit

**Decision:** Give every shot one primary visual beat that a viewer can describe in one sentence immediately after seeing it.

Secondary motion may support the beat but may not compete with it. When several actions matter, choose the one that changes the story and either remove or defer the others.

A single beat still contains several independent systems. The next decision is to stop describing camera and subject behavior as though they were one movement.
