# C13-007: Replace Ambiguous Directions With Observable Geometry

**Method:** YY Method™ Home Edition v2.3 — Capture → Why → Why-Not → Commit → Timestamp

**Status:** Decided

**Date:** 2026-07-24

**Domain:** Scene Composition

**Depends On:** C13-006

**Freshness Boundary:** Active. Revisit if future systems consistently translate narrative direction into the intended body orientation and screen geometry.

**Source:** Public-safe synthesis from iterative AI video production. Exact prompts, generated clips, reference libraries, and platform-specific production fixtures remain private.

---

## Capture

Several scenes required a character to leave.

Prompts used words such as leaving, departing, walking away, or heading toward the exit. The action remained inconsistent. A person could move away while facing the building, wave toward the wrong side, or visually appear to be arriving.

The narrative verb was correct. The image geometry was not.

---

## Why

Humans infer the physical arrangement contained in a narrative action.

Leaving a house normally implies facing away from the door and moving toward the path. The verb seemed to contain the necessary direction.

---

## Why-Not

The generator still had to decide body orientation, direction of travel, destination, camera side, and where the wave was aimed.

Different choices could all satisfy the abstract word while producing opposite readings.

The scar was discovering that intention is not geometry.

---

## Commit

**Decision:** Describe directional actions through observable spatial relationships.

State that the subject faces the walkway, has their back partially toward the house, moves away from the front door, and waves over one shoulder while continuing forward.

Specify body orientation and travel direction independently when both matter.

At this point the story, movement, and behavior are clearer, but the generator still reinvents who and what appears in the frame. The next phase removes those decisions.
