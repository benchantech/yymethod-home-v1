# C13-016: Optimize for Repeatability Rather Than Individual Success

**Method:** YY Method™ Home Edition v2.3 — Capture → Why → Why-Not → Commit → Timestamp

**Status:** Decided

**Date:** 2026-07-24

**Domain:** Production Strategy

**Depends On:** C13-015

**Freshness Boundary:** Active. Revisit if generation becomes sufficiently deterministic that an isolated success is automatically reproducible.

**Source:** Public-safe synthesis from iterative AI video production. Exact prompts, generated clips, reference libraries, and platform-specific production fixtures remain private.

---

## Capture

Many sessions ended with a good clip and no durable explanation.

When a similar shot was needed later, the earlier success could not be reproduced without rediscovery. The project accumulated excellent exceptions faster than dependable methods.

---

## Why

A successful output feels complete.

Continuing to investigate after the clip works appears wasteful when another scene is waiting.

---

## Why-Not

One successful generation proves only that success occurred under one set of conditions.

Without identifying and preserving the underlying decision, the next scene returns to experimentation. The production system remains dependent on memory and luck.

The scar was learning that isolated quality does not scale.

---

## Commit

**Decision:** Evaluate improvements by whether they can be repeated across scenes, not merely by whether they produced one strong clip.

When a technique works, identify the governing decision, record its scope, and incorporate it into the standard workflow.

Repeatability turns production into infrastructure. The remaining question is what form that knowledge should take as tools and prompts change.
